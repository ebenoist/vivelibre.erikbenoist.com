In ancient times, when the boundaries of Cantinil were drawn, people camped in this place, our home, and left a seed of avocado and durazno. In the year 1915 our grandparents came to live under the avocado tree and durazno tree, and for this our home has been named “El Durazno” since that time.


This land is a cold place where there is no coffee, banana, and other crops that could improve the economy. We are 4 kilometers away from the nearest community. Our publeo has slowly grown smaller as many families have left in search of more fertile land and since that time only 13 families are left in El Durazno.


Our grandparents planted corn and beans without fertilizer because the soils were so fertile. Our grandparents ground the corn for tortillas with stone, because they do not know of any other kind of mill. They carried water in clay pots, tending to chickens, pigs, sheep, goats, and cattle which helped them to survive. They would transport their products, such as eggs and chickens, to the market on foot to Huehuetenango. It took two days to arrive and two to return, always in bare feet and very few clothes, because there was no money to buy them.


Some families migrated to the south, to the coast, leaving their homes abandoned and taking with them their children. It took 8 days to arrive, and 8 to return.

Years later, the culture of sharing land to raise animals such as sheep, goats, and cattle changed, and many people prohibited us from bringing animals onto their land, and so our life continued to become more difficult.


Our parents continued to live, in this place, as always, planting corn and beans, vegetables, wheat, and taking care of chickens and pigs. Life, though, did become easier because they knew how to use hand mills and bring in water by pipe. Our nearest market was in San Antonio Huista, which was a day’s journey; leaving at 2 o’clock in the morning and returning late in the afternoon. Our parents went out to earn money near lake lemon for some time, and carried with them 100 to 150 pounds from those places, some used some strap sandals and others barefooted.


Some time later there were better opportunities, roads, schools, and closer market. But life, in this community, is always a difficult life because without chemical fertilizers we can not produce enough to eat. We work hard to create compost and we, the women of El Durazno, help support the men by planting vegetables.


We believe they are chances of having fruit trees some day, but because of our poverty can not purchase the seed; our men barely live on daily wages.

Today in this community, we have some services such as electricity, a road, and a school. And, as always, we keep on living by selling poultry in nearby communities, or when it is season the women, children, and men work picking coffee, and other fieldwork.


We are organized, and we desire nothing more than some better jobs so as to better our lives.


El Durazno 12/10/2,010
