Located high in the mountains of Unión Cantinil, El Durazno is a small community of 75 people whom struggle daily to subsist. The farmers here travel far during the coffee harvest to try and seek daily labor wages that barely top $5 a day, hauling 100lbs bags of coffee up steep mountain inclines. While most of Unión Cantinil can count on the harvest to yearly bring enough to eat, El Durazno, at 7,217 feet is far too high to grow coffee, and without easy access, the people subsist on a meager corn harvest, and small green peaches where the name El Durazno comes from.


Working with the Peace Corps, our projects in this often ignored area, include a woman’s cooperative working with chickens and a project that will bring peach and avocado trees to this stunning area, but the people of El Durazno are in a cycle of extreme poverty. While productive projects like fruit trees and chicken coops should help in the long run, without getting basic necessities up to standard levels the children in this area, like their parents, will continue to suffer.


This is why we are asking you to help us out. Of the 12 homes in El Durazno only two have something other than a dirt floor. Two homes have no stove and the families cook on an open flame in the middle of the home. I believe this is an injustice that, together, we can correct. Each stove will cost $150 and every floor will cost around $215 depending, of course, on size differences, grading, etc... Our goal is to put together enough money to install a floor and a stove in every home, a goal of $2,500.


Together we can make a real difference in the lives of these warm wonderful people. How often is it that you have the opportunity to so directly make a difference? I will update all donors on our progress, and please know that 100% of every donation will go directly into improving the community. And just because every project needs to start somewhere, I will be personally donating the first stove. If I can manage to scrape together enough, I hope you can as well.


Thank you on the behalf of El Durazno,

Erik Benoist
